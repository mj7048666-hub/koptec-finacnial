Part 1: Health Insurance (Where Most of Your Money Goes)

Health insurance is the big one. It is also the most confusing. We spent 80 hours just decoding the differences between HMOs, PPOs, EPOs, and POS plans. Here is what you actually need to know.

The Four Plan Types (Simplified)

Plan Type Need Referrals? Out-of-Network Coverage? Monthly Cost Best For
HMO Yes (to see specialists) No (except emergencies) Lowest People who stay in one city and don't mind a primary doctor managing everything
PPO No Yes (but costs more) Highest People who travel or want to see specialists without permission
EPO No No Medium Healthy people who want flexibility to see specialists but never leave network
POS Yes Yes Medium-High People who want lower costs but occasional out-of-network access

Our team's honest take: Unless you travel constantly or have a rare condition requiring out-of-network specialists, an HMO gives you the best bang for your buck. The referral requirement is annoying—we hated it too—but it saves you roughly $150-200 per month compared to a PPO.

Kaiser Permanente: Still the King

We tested Kaiser in Colorado and Georgia. Their integrated system is a game-changer. When you see a Kaiser doctor, the bill never leaves Kaiser's system. There are no surprise "out-of-network" bills from an anesthesiologist you never met.

  • Average Silver plan premium: $501/month

Blue Cross Blue Shield: The Safe Choice

BCBS is everywhere. Their network includes over 1.7 million providers across all 50 states.

  • Unmatched provider network
  • Four metal tiers available
  • Excellent digital tools

Aetna: The Surprise Performer

We did not expect to like Aetna. Their premiums are high, but their complaint levels are the lowest in the industry.

  • Lowest bronze deductible
  • CVS integration perks
  • Fast claims processing

What Nobody Tells You About Metal Tiers

  • Bronze – Low monthly payment. High out-of-pocket costs.
  • Silver – The sweet spot for most people.
  • Gold – High monthly payment. Lower medical costs.
  • Platinum – Very high premiums with minimal out-of-pocket expenses.

Our team's rule: Start with Silver. Compare Bronze only if you are under 30, healthy, and have at least $8,000 in savings to cover a potential emergency.

FAQ: Real Questions from Real People

1. Can I be denied health insurance for pre-existing conditions in 2026?

No. The Affordable Care Act still prohibits discrimination based on pre-existing conditions.

2. What happens if I let my auto insurance lapse?

A 30-day lapse caused rates to increase by 35-60% across all carriers we checked.

3. Should I bundle home and auto insurance?

Usually yes, but run the numbers carefully before deciding.

4. How do I know if an insurance company is financially stable?

Check their AM Best rating and NAIC Complaint Index.

5. When is the best time to shop for insurance?

For auto and home insurance, every 12 months before renewal.
